My Experience at Groupon So Far

Jul 30th, 2011

Today marks the end of my third week working at Groupon as an intern/apprentice. The first week I was with the engineering support team, getting a feel for the application, writing my first tiny bits of code and seeing how support tickets were handled. The second week I worked on the front end, getting a feel for that side of the application and this past week I have spent working with Groupon touch, the mobile version of the Groupon website.

So far Groupon has been absolutely awesome, I love it there! Having a fun work environment makes all the difference when it comes to being motivated to work hard. Appreciating your employers and what they do for you makes you want to contribute back so much more.

Working on the mobile version of Groupon has exposed me to the reality that the page I’ve been building will shortly be displayed to millions of users who view Groupon on their mobile or tablet device. It seems like a small project when I’m sitting there with a few tiny files make some changes in the HTML and CSS but that page along with the whole touch version will soon be viewed by an enormous user base — pretty cool.

Along with the above, it’s also a very exciting time to be there considering Groupon’s insurmountable amount of media attention lately. I came at the right time just about when all the action is taking place. My first week we released our partnership with Expedia to provide travel deals. We also just released our partnership with foursquare today. Although a bit behind some of the other Daily Deal sites (i.e. LivingSocial and Gilt Groupe), our partnership with them is different because we also include Now! Deals, which allows users to redeem deals immediately when they check into a location, unlike the other deal sites who do not provide immediate redemption.

On a final note, the last thing I want to say is how much I’ve come to appreciate the humor of Groupon. Some of the emails between customer service and customers are absolutely hilarious as well as the multitude of other humorous jokes or situations we’ve been in.

Looking forward to the coming weeks there!
